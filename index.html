About

Read about my journey.

  • Fall 2012

    Alice Lloyd College

    I spent a semester at Alice Lloyd College. ALC is located in Knott County, Ky deep in the Appalachian mountains. The school is highly conservative and very secluded. These factors lead to my stay being short and me seeking other avenues of success.

  • Spring 2013

    United States Army

    After leaving Alice Lloyd I needed to do something to advance my future. I am from a very small mountain community in Eastern Ky called Bledsoe. As such there is a scarcity of employment and after my time at ALC I felt college wasn't for me. My great-grandfather and uncle had both served in the Army so why couldn't I? I was accepted and placed as a 35-F(Intelligence Analyst). I completed basic training and was about to go to AIT when we got word that due a budget closure many of us would be going home I was one of those unfortunate enough to be chosen.

  • Summer 2013 - Fall 2015

    The Struggle Years

    This was the roughest time of my adventure in adulthood. I had felt failure both at Alice Lloyd and the Army. I enrolled in community college, but didn't put much effort into it as I felt defeated and lost. I would pass just enough to stay in school until the 2015 Spring semester when my failures caught up and I lost my financial aid benefits. Now unable to go to school and unable to serve in the Army I had to find work.

  • August 2015

    Pine Mountain Settlement School

    I went on the hunt. I was applying for any job I could find an opening for. One of these was an AmeriCorps VISTA position at Pine Mountain Settlement School. I sent in an application and less than a week later got a response. The Executive Director of PMSS called me and regretfully informed me that he felt I wouldn't be a good fit for the position. The first cahnce I had at a job gone in an instant. Then he started speaking again and with what he said I found hope. My skills were going to be useful to him. He needed someone to help with technology related issues they were having at PMSS. He wanted me to come in the next day and meet with him and start work soon. I was finally getting somewhere!

  • Late Spring 2015 - Current

    The Switch Up

    I started working for PMSS. I fixed computers and installed WiFi routers. I did troubleshooting on printers and rebooted desktops. I was doing something I loved and making money doing it! My boss then comes and asks me to build a website for the school. I had never really done anything like that before, but he believed I could do it. So I did. He then said he heard of a program called TEKY where they were going to be hiring people to train them in mobile app development and then employing them. I applied and got accepted as 1 out of around 1,000 applicants. Only a total of 50 were chosen. I went through the training and got accepted into the internship phase. I finished that and now I am looking at employment oppurtunities in multiple companies. From a rough beginning I came out a coder with a refined marketable skill I could have employment and a career in.

GiftIt iOS

Charity in your pocket.

GiftIt is an application that allows any group to manage charitable transactions within a closed system. Groups can provide assistance through GiftIt as a member to member donation system overseen by a managing body. Individuals can be anonymous or have their name associated with needs and donations. In essence GiftIt is a rapidly deployable mobile charity management system with the ability to be rebranded and published with minimum cost to organizations.
